PERSONAL SECURITY

In 2010, we were more able to use the state and the European Union offered assistance in the form of activation workers, as is evident from the contribution from the Center for Revenue and Utilities, presented in part of revenues. Especially at the end of the year, these workers formed a team that significantly improved animal care. It remains to be hoped that this assistance will also be provided at other times as it is a prerequisite for the operation of the shelter. The volunteers who help with their abilities and possibilities and who sacrifice their free time and energy for the animals themselves and for love are a great help for the shelter. They arrive in the late afternoon and evening hours at the shelter to allow the animals to "expel" the drug, to check if they are okay. Others work from home, even from another city, providing home care. Others regularly help in stock exchanges, It is a great help to set up a Facebook that our Sympathicist from Bratislava leads with great ingenuity. The only reward for all these people is the feeling that the animals are secure, healthy, healthy, and most likely in a new, loving home.

In March and April, the dangerous tiles were replaced by concrete pavements alongside the cotercoats, the floor was laid in the social room, the deadlocks were repaired on the shelter between the cells, the areas under the garage and the quarters were being quarried in the quarantine area and in front of the premises, - raw and unimobuniek. A complete reconstruction of the work was done also with coats of roofs and unimobuniek - for this work the City of Piešťany provided a subsidy of 500 €. In May, the work was weather-proof - heavy rain with a strong wind would have sparked the entire process, which would have resulted in great damage to property and possibly human and animal health. In May, new lattice grilles were installed on windows and doors of unimobuniek, a waste pipe was installed on the front premises. During the summer months, maintenance and repairs were carried out by our own staff. VETERINARY TREATMENT

The cost of veterinary treatment exceeded all current limits in 2010, reaching a height of over 21 thousand. €. Besides the reasons we have already explained in the annual reports of previous years, we have caused an increase of approx. € chip purchase for nationwide chipping of animals, which we started in the end of last year. We believe that this step will contribute in particular to the discovery of irresponsible owners and thus to the decrease in the number of abandoned and abused animals. In cooperation with the Lower Silesian Region, we continue the effort to reduce the number of unwanted youngsters by sterilization and sterilization of all animals, allowing for their age and health. Not every adopter approves this action, but we do not see another humane way of solving a problem that the competent authorities have ignored for at least twenty years. Of the animals we have taken to the shelter, were in 2010 sterilized, respectively. castrated 156 dogs and 23 cats. In 2010 we received 557 animals, of which 469 dogs and 88 cats. Of this number, 61 animals returned to their original owners, 16 died and 13 were euthanized. The remaining 449 animals, of which 392 dogs and 57 cats, came to new homes.

COOPERATION WITH FOREIGNERS

We are delighted that cooperation with foreign partners, individuals and protection groups from Austria and Germany continues and our partners from older times are returning to us, who have encouraged positive changes in the shelter to work together. An important act is to include our shelter in the TR @ CES Internet system, which creates a network between the veterinary authorities of the EU Member States and economic operators with Decision 2002/459 / EC. Animals leaving our shelter abroad are registered in this system, which was created to protect, track, record and target the illegal export of animals.
